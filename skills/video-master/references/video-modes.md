# Video Modes

Confirm the video mode before writing scripts or shot lists. The mode controls rhythm, script structure, audio strategy, storyboard coverage, and final deliverables.

## Mode Table

| Mode | Use When | Rhythm | Script Focus | Audio Focus | Storyboard Coverage |
| ---- | -------- | ------ | ------------ | ----------- | ------------------ |
| `fast-paced-tvc` | Commercial ad, launch spot, strong brand/product memory | Short non-uniform shots, strong first 1-3s hook, energetic proof beats | Slogan, benefit, product proof, CTA | Music beat, punchy VO, sound design hits | Usually every shot or key shot |
| `product-promo-short` | Social product promo or seeding video | Moderate pacing, proof shots get room | Problem, product, use case, result | Clear VO and captions | Every shot when <=20 |
| `narrative-short` | Story-led short film or emotional ad | Longer character shots, fewer cuts | Character, conflict, turn, payoff | Dialogue, ambience, emotional music | Key story beats |
| `animation` | Animated character, explainer, stylized visual | Depends on animation style; action beats need clear staging | World, character, action, transformation | VO, music, motion cues | Character/design frames first |
| `tutorial-explainer` | How-to, educational, step-by-step product demo | Step-based, readable, not too fast | Clarity, sequence, instruction | Voiceover and captions dominate | Key procedural steps |
| `brand-film` | Brand image, values, mood piece | Slower, breathing shots, visual memory | Atmosphere, values, emotional associations | Music and ambience | Key frames, hero frames |
| `ecommerce-conversion-short` | Direct conversion, live-commerce style, offer video | Fast start, proof/offer/CTA cycles | Price, benefit, proof, urgency | Strong captions and CTA | Product and proof frames |

## Rhythm Rules

- Do not default to equal shot durations.
- Use director intent, not table symmetry. A strong rhythm map should mix impact cuts, proof beats, breath shots, and payoff shots according to the subject.
- For advertising modes, assign shot lengths by function:
  - Hook: usually 1-3 seconds.
  - Product reveal/proof: usually 2-5 seconds.
  - Usage or transformation: usually 4-7 seconds.
  - CTA: usually 2-4 seconds.
- For story modes, let emotion and action determine shot length.
- For tutorial modes, use step clarity instead of speed.
- For high-motion subjects such as racing, sport, chase, action, or a fast launch film, include at least one rapid-cut cluster. A typical cluster can be `0.6s / 0.8s / 1.2s / 2s`, followed by a longer shot that lets the audience feel the consequence.
- High-motion shot lists should name camera energy per shot: handheld, vehicle-mounted vibration, impact shake, whip pan, hard push-in, POV, pass-by, or locked-off.
- Do not spread a one-minute high-motion film into only 8-10 long shots unless the concept is deliberately meditative and the user confirmed that choice.

## Mode-Specific Deliverables

- TVC/commercial: `slogan_bank.md`, copy-ready video prompts, CTA options.
- Product promo: product proof shots, benefit hierarchy, use-case captions.
- Narrative short: scene script, dialogue, character continuity notes.
- Animation: character sheet prompts, world/style bible, action staging.
- Tutorial: voiceover script, steps, captions, diagram/insert prompts.
- Brand film: mood board prompts, hero frames, music direction.
- Ecommerce: offer copy, selling point cards, CTA variants.
